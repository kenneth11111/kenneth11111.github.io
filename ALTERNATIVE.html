About the New York City Council

The New York City Council is the law-making body of New York City. The Council monitors the operation and performance of city agencies, makes land use decisions, and approves the city's budget.

The Council is an equal partner with the Mayor in the governance of the city. It is made up of 51 members, each elected from a separate district across the five boroughs for a four-year term.

Key Responsibilities

  • Introducing and passing legislation
  • Approving the city's budget
  • Monitoring city agencies
  • Making land use decisions
  • Overseeing city programs
