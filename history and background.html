Welcome to Kayunga Town Council

Kayunga Town Council is the local government body responsible for the administration and development of Kayunga Town in Central Uganda

Our Background

Kayunga Town Council is located in Kayunga District, Central Uganda, approximately 74 kilometers northeast of Kampala, the capital city. The town sits on the northern banks of the River Nile, making it a strategically important location for trade and commerce.

Established as a town council in 2000, Kayunga has grown from a small trading center to a bustling urban center serving as the administrative and commercial hub of Kayunga District. The town council oversees an area of approximately 28 square kilometers with a growing population that reflects Uganda's rapid urbanization.

Our jurisdiction covers both the urban center and surrounding areas, with a focus on providing essential services, infrastructure development, and promoting economic growth while preserving our cultural heritage and natural resources.

Our Mandate & Responsibilities

As a local government entity, Kayunga Town Council is mandated to provide services and governance to residents within its jurisdiction. Our core responsibilities include:

  • Urban planning and development control
  • Provision and maintenance of public infrastructure
  • Public health and sanitation services
  • Revenue collection and financial management
  • Business licensing and regulation
  • Education and community services
  • Environmental protection and management

We work in partnership with central government ministries, development partners, and community organizations to deliver these services effectively and efficiently.

Our Historical Journey

Trace the evolution of Kayunga from a small trading center to a thriving town council

Pre-1900

Early Settlement

Kayunga began as a small fishing and farming community along the banks of the River Nile. The area was primarily inhabited by the Baganda people with significant influence from the Busoga Kingdom.

1900-1950

Colonial Administration

During the British colonial period, Kayunga served as an administrative outpost. The area developed as a minor trading center for cotton and coffee, which were the main cash crops in the region.

1962-1980

Post-Independence Growth

After Uganda's independence in 1962, Kayunga continued to grow as a trading center. The town benefited from its strategic location along the Kampala-Jinja highway, facilitating trade between different regions.

2000

Town Council Status

Kayunga was officially granted Town Council status, marking a significant milestone in its development. This recognition came with increased administrative responsibilities and funding for urban development.

2006

District Headquarters

With the creation of Kayunga District, the town council became the district headquarters, further elevating its administrative importance and accelerating infrastructure development.

2010-Present

Modern Development

Recent years have seen significant infrastructure improvements, including road networks, market modernization, and public facilities. The town continues to grow as a commercial and administrative hub while facing challenges of rapid urbanization.
