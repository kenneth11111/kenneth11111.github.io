Government Empowerment Programs

Kayunga Town Council facilitates the implementation of key government programs designed to empower youth, women, and communities through sustainable livelihood initiatives, agricultural development, and social protection.

These programs are part of the Government of Uganda's broader strategy to eradicate poverty, enhance household incomes, and promote inclusive socio-economic transformation.

Government Empowerment Programs

Key initiatives transforming lives in Kayunga Town Council

Youth Livelihood Programme (YLP)

A flagship government program providing startup capital for youth groups to engage in income-generating activities and enterprise development.

Program Guidelines

  • Targets youth aged 18-30 years in registered groups
  • Maximum group size: 10-15 members
  • Revolving fund model with 10% member contribution
  • Focus on agriculture, trade, services and small-scale industries
  • Repayment period: 1-2 years with grace period

How to Apply

Visit the Community Development Office at Kayunga Town Council with:

  • Group registration certificate
  • Membership list with LC1 verification
  • Detailed project proposal
  • Group constitution and minutes

Uganda Women Entrepreneurship Programme (UWEP)

Empowering women through access to interest-free credit, skills development, and appropriate technologies for enterprise growth.

Program Guidelines

  • Exclusively for women aged 18+ years
  • Group size: 10-15 registered women
  • Interest-free revolving fund
  • Focus on viable income-generating activities
  • Mandatory savings component

How to Apply

Submit application through the Gender Office with:

  • Women group registration documents
  • Business plan for proposed enterprise
  • Group bank account details
  • LC1 recommendation letter

Parish Development Model (PDM)

A strategy for organizing and delivering public and private sector interventions for wealth creation and employment generation at the parish level.

Program Guidelines

  • Targets households in subsistence economy
  • Seven pillars: Production, Infrastructure, Financial Inclusion
  • Parish-based planning and implementation
  • SACCO model for financial inclusion
  • Focus on agricultural value chains

How to Apply

Register with your Parish SACCO through:

  • Visit your parish chief's office
  • Join existing SACCO or form new one
  • Participate in parish planning meetings
  • Submit household enterprise proposal

Social Assistance Grants for Empowerment (SAGE)

Direct income support for vulnerable groups including older persons, persons with disabilities, and vulnerable families to improve their livelihoods.

Program Guidelines

  • Older persons: 80+ years (65+ in Karamoja)
  • Persons with severe disabilities
  • Vulnerable families as identified
  • Quarterly cash transfers
  • Complementary livelihood support

How to Apply

Registration through Community Development Office:

  • National ID for age verification
  • Medical report for disability claims
  • LC1 recommendation for vulnerable families
  • Household vulnerability assessment

Agricultural Development & Food Security Programs

National initiatives to enhance agricultural productivity, promote food security, and support farmers with inputs, extension services, and market access.

Program Guidelines

  • Distribution of improved seeds and inputs
  • Farmer training and extension services
  • Post-harvest handling support
  • Market access and value addition
  • Climate-smart agriculture practices

How to Apply

Register with the Production Department:

  • Farmer group registration
  • Land ownership/access verification
  • Crop/livestock enterprise plan
  • Participation in farmer training sessions

Program Eligibility

Requirements for accessing government empowerment programs

Youth Livelihood Programme

  • Youth aged 18-30 years
  • Formed group of 10-15 members
  • Group registered with local council
  • Viable project proposal
  • Willingness to contribute 10% of project cost

UWEP

  • Women aged 18+ years
  • Formed group of 10-15 women
  • Group registered with local council
  • Viable income-generating activity
  • Willingness to save and reinvest

Parish Development Model

  • Households in the subsistence economy
  • Membership in parish SACCO
  • Engagement in one of the 7 pillars
  • Resident in the parish
  • Willingness to participate in parish planning

How to Apply

Step-by-step process for accessing government empowerment programs

1

Group Formation

Form a group of 10-15 members with shared interests and register with your local council.

2

Project Development

Develop a viable project proposal with clear objectives, activities, and budget.

3

Submit Application

Submit your application through the community development office at Kayunga Town Council.

4

Verification & Approval

Your application will be verified and approved by the relevant committee.

5

Receive Support

Successful groups receive funding, training, and ongoing mentorship.
