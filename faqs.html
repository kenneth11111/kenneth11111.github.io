Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about our services, procedures, and policies

Services

How do I report a public health concern?

You can report public health concerns by:

  • Calling our Public Health Department at +256 414 231 200
  • Visiting our offices during working hours (Mon-Fri, 8:00AM - 5:00PM)
  • Using our online service request form on the website
  • Contacting your local council representative
What are the waste collection schedules in my area?

Waste collection schedules vary by zone:

  • Central Business District: Daily collection
  • Residential Areas: Twice weekly (Mondays & Thursdays)
  • Outskirts: Weekly collection (Wednesdays)

For specific schedules in your area, please contact the Waste Management Department.

How do I report road damage or maintenance issues?

To report road damage or maintenance issues:

  1. Take a clear photo of the issue if possible
  2. Note the exact location (street name, landmarks)
  3. Call our Roads Department at 0750858803
  4. Submit a service request through our website
  5. Visit our offices with the details

Emergency road issues are prioritized for immediate attention.

Business & Licensing

What documents do I need to apply for a business license?

Required documents for business license application:

  • Completed application form
  • National ID or passport
  • Passport-size photographs (2)
  • Proof of business address (rental agreement or utility bill)
  • Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  • Business registration certificate (for companies)
  • Health inspection certificate (for food-related businesses)
How long does it take to process a business license?

The processing time for business licenses varies:

  • Standard Processing: 7-10 working days
  • Express Processing: 3-5 working days (additional fee applies)
  • Complex Applications: Up to 14 working days

Processing time begins once all required documents are submitted and verified.

Can I operate my business while waiting for license approval?

No, you cannot legally operate your business without a valid license from Kayunga Town Council. Operating without a license may result in:

  • Fines and penalties
  • Business closure
  • Legal action

We recommend applying for your license well in advance of your planned opening date.

Payments & Fees

What payment methods are accepted for council fees?

We accept multiple payment methods for your convenience:

  • Cash: At our offices during working hours
  • Bank Transfer: To our designated bank accounts
  • Mobile Money: MTN Mobile Money and Airtel Money
  • Online Payment: Through our secure payment portal

Always request and keep your payment receipt as proof of transaction.

How can I check my outstanding bills or fees?

You can check your outstanding bills through:

  • Our online customer portal (requires registration)
  • SMS service: Send your account number to 6666
  • Visiting our finance department with your account details
  • Calling our customer service line: +256 414 231 200

All property owners are encouraged to register for online services for easier access.

What happens if I pay my fees late?

Late payments may incur penalties:

  • 1-30 days late: 10% penalty on outstanding amount
  • 31-60 days late: 20% penalty on outstanding amount
  • Over 60 days late: Additional legal actions may be taken

If you're experiencing financial difficulties, please contact our finance department to discuss payment arrangements.

Physical Planning & Development Applications

What is a development application?

A development application is a formal request submitted to the Town Council for permission to carry out development works such as construction, renovation, change of land use, or subdivision of land.

All development applications must comply with the Physical Planning Act and the Kayunga Town Council Physical Development Plans.

When is development permission required?

Development permission is required for:

  • Erection of new buildings or structures
  • Extension, alteration, or renovation of existing buildings
  • Change of use of land or buildings
  • Subdivision or consolidation of land
  • Excavation or filling of land
  • Display of advertisements/signage
  • Temporary structures and uses
What documents are required for a development application?

The following documents are typically required:

  • Completed application form
  • Copy of land title or proof of ownership
  • Location plan (1:2500 scale)
  • Site plan (1:500 scale)
  • Architectural drawings (floor plans, elevations, sections)
  • Structural drawings and calculations
  • Land survey plan
  • Environmental Impact Assessment (where applicable)
  • Proof of payment of application fees
How long does the development application process take?

The processing time varies depending on the complexity of the application:

  • Simple applications: 4-6 weeks
  • Medium complexity: 6-8 weeks
  • Complex applications: 8-12 weeks or more
  • Applications requiring EIA: Additional 4-6 weeks

The timeline begins once all required documents have been submitted and the application is deemed complete.

What are the fees for development applications?

Development application fees are calculated based on:

  • Type of development
  • Size/scale of the project
  • Estimated construction cost
  • Location of the development

A detailed fee structure is available at the Physical Planning Department. Fees are payable upon submission of the application and are non-refundable.

Can I start construction before getting approval?

No, it is illegal to commence any development without prior approval from the Town Council.

Commencing development without approval may result in:

  • Stop-work orders
  • Demolition of unauthorized structures
  • Heavy fines and penalties
  • Legal prosecution
  • Difficulty in obtaining future approvals

Development Applications

What is the process for submitting a development application?

The development application process involves the following steps:

  1. Pre-application consultation with the Physical Planning Department
  2. Preparation of required documents and plans
  3. Submission of the complete application package
  4. Initial review and validation of documents
  5. Technical assessment by relevant departments
  6. Site inspection by planning officials
  7. Review and decision by the Planning Committee
  8. Issuance of development permission or rejection letter
What happens if my application is incomplete?

If your application is incomplete:

  • You will receive a notice specifying the missing documents or information
  • The processing timeline will be paused until all requirements are met
  • You have 30 days to submit the missing items, after which your application may be considered withdrawn
  • Application fees are non-refundable if the application is withdrawn or rejected due to incompleteness
Can I amend my development application after submission?

Yes, you can request amendments to your development application, but:

  • Minor amendments may be accepted during the review process
  • Major changes may require withdrawal of the original application and submission of a new one
  • Additional fees may apply for processing amendments
  • The processing timeline may be extended for amended applications
What is the validity period of an approved development permission?

Development permissions are valid for specific periods:

  • Standard permissions: 2 years from the date of approval
  • Large-scale developments: 3 years from the date of approval
  • Subdivision approvals: 3 years from the date of approval

You may apply for an extension before the expiry date, subject to approval and payment of extension fees.

What should I do if my application is rejected?

If your application is rejected:

  • You will receive a written explanation of the reasons for rejection
  • You may address the issues identified and resubmit the application
  • You have the right to appeal the decision within 30 days of notification
  • The appeal process involves submitting a formal appeal to the Town Clerk with supporting documents
Do I need separate approvals from other agencies?

Depending on your project, you may need additional approvals from:

  • National Water and Sewerage Corporation (NWSC) for water and sewerage connections
  • Uganda Electricity Distribution Company Limited (UEDCL) for power supply
  • National Environment Management Authority (NEMA) for environmental compliance
  • Ministry of Lands, Housing and Urban Development for certain land transactions
  • Uganda National Roads Authority (UNRA) for developments affecting major roads

It is your responsibility to obtain all necessary approvals before commencing development.

What is the role of the Physical Planning Committee?

The Physical Planning Committee:

  • Reviews development applications and makes decisions on approval or rejection
  • Ensures compliance with physical planning laws and regulations
  • Considers public input and technical assessments
  • May impose conditions on approved developments
  • Hears appeals on development decisions

The Committee typically meets monthly to consider applications.

Are there different types of development applications?

Yes, there are several types of development applications:

  • Outline Permission: For establishing the principle of development
  • Detailed Permission: For full approval of a specific development
  • Change of Use: For changing the use of land or buildings
  • Subdivision: For dividing land into smaller parcels
  • Extension/Renovation: For extending or renovating existing structures
  • Advertisement: For display of signs and advertisements
What are the consequences of developing without permission?

Developing without permission is an offense that may result in:

  • Issuance of an enforcement notice requiring you to stop work
  • Demolition of unauthorized structures at your expense
  • Substantial fines and legal prosecution
  • Difficulty in obtaining future planning permissions
  • Inability to obtain occupancy certificates for the development

It is always advisable to obtain proper permission before commencing any development.

How can I check the status of my development application?

You can check your application status through:

  • Our online application tracking system (if available)
  • Visiting the Physical Planning Department with your application reference number
  • Calling the Planning Department at +256 414 231 200
  • Emailing the department at planning@kayungatc.go.ug

Please have your application reference number ready when making inquiries.

  • Participate in public consultations for major projects
  • Contact your local council representative with suggestions
  • Volunteer for community clean-up and development initiatives

Your input is valuable in shaping the future of Kayunga Town.

  • Radio announcements on local stations
