About Kampala Capital City Authority

The Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA) is the governing body of Kampala, the capital city of Uganda. It was established by the Ugandan Parliament in 2010 with the mandate to manage and administer the city, replacing the former Kampala City Council.

KCCA is responsible for the overall development and management of Kampala, including infrastructure development, service delivery, urban planning, revenue collection, and enforcement of city laws and regulations. The Authority works to transform Kampala into a vibrant, attractive, and sustainable city.

Our Mandate

The mandate of KCCA is derived from the Kampala Capital City Act, 2010. The Authority is tasked with:

  • Providing and maintaining city infrastructure and services
  • Promoting and facilitating economic development
  • Ensuring a clean and healthy environment
  • Maintaining law and order in the city
  • Collecting revenues due to the Authority

Our Mission

To deliver quality services to the people of Kampala and foster sustainable socio-economic development through inclusive and accountable governance.

Our Vision

A vibrant, attractive and sustainable city with a skilled and prosperous population.

Our History

Kampala's local government has evolved significantly over the years. The city was first declared a township in 1906 and later became a municipality in 1949. In 1967, Kampala became a city, administered by the Kampala City Council.

In 2010, the Ugandan Parliament enacted the Kampala Capital City Act, which established the Kampala Capital City Authority. This new structure was designed to address governance challenges and improve service delivery in the city. The Authority commenced operations in March 2011.

Since its establishment, KCCA has implemented numerous reforms and development projects aimed at transforming Kampala into a modern, well-planned city with improved infrastructure and services for its residents.

Organizational Structure

The Kampala Capital City Authority is structured to effectively deliver its mandate. The organization is headed by the Executive Director, who is responsible for the day-to-day administration of the Authority.

KCCA operates through various directorates and departments, including:

  • Directorate of Engineering and Technical Services
  • Directorate of Public Health and Environment
  • Directorate of Gender, Community Services and Production
  • Directorate of Revenue Collection
  • Directorate of Administration and Human Resources
  • Directorate of Internal Audit
  • Directorate of Legal Affairs

The political leadership is provided by the Lord Mayor and elected councilors who represent the various divisions and special interest groups in the city.
